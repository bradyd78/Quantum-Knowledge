## 1. Dirac Notation

üì¶ **Dirac Notation ‚Äî In Juice Box Terms**

Dirac notation uses ‚Äúkets‚Äù and ‚Äúbras‚Äù to describe quantum states:

- **Ket (|œà‚ü©)** ‚Üí ‚ÄúShould be‚Äù ‚Äî like labeling a juice box, e.g. ‚ÄúThis is supposed to be apple juice.‚Äù
- **Bra (‚ü®œà|)** ‚Üí ‚ÄúSeems like‚Äù ‚Äî tasting it to check, e.g. ‚ÄúDoes this act like apple juice?‚Äù
- **Bra‚Äìket (‚ü®œà|œà‚ü©)** ‚Üí ‚ÄúI‚Äôm this sure‚Äù ‚Äî measuring how likely it actually is apple juice.

It‚Äôs a way to represent and compare quantum states using inner products.
Succinctly: **Ket = identity, Bra = evaluation, Bra‚Äìket = confidence.**

## 2. Linear Algebra

# üß† Introduction to Linear Algebra (Quantum Computing Focus)

Linear algebra is the mathematical foundation of quantum computing. It deals with vectors, matrices, and transformations ‚Äî all of which are essential for understanding quantum states, gates, and measurements.

---

## üìö Why Learn Linear Algebra?

Quantum computing uses:
- **Vectors** ‚Üí to represent quantum states (`|œà‚ü©`)
- **Matrices** ‚Üí to represent quantum gates
- **Inner products** ‚Üí to calculate probabilities
- **Tensor products** ‚Üí to combine qubits
- **Eigenvalues/eigenvectors** ‚Üí to understand quantum measurements

---

## üß© Key Concepts

| Concept                  | Description                                         | Quantum Relevance                        |
|--------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| **Vector**               | A list of numbers (e.g., `[1, 0]`)                  | Represents quantum states like `|0‚ü©`     |
| **Matrix**               | A grid of numbers that transforms vectors           | Quantum gates are matrices               |
| **Matrix Multiplication**| Applying a transformation to a vector               | Simulates quantum circuits               |
| **Inner Product**        | Measures similarity between vectors                 | Determines probabilities of outcomes     |
| **Eigenvalues/Eigenvectors** | Special vectors that don‚Äôt change direction     | Key to quantum measurements              |
| **Complex Numbers**      | Numbers with real and imaginary parts               | Quantum amplitudes are complex           |
| **Unitary/Hermitian Matrices** | Preserve length and have real eigenvalues   | Describe quantum gates and observables   |
